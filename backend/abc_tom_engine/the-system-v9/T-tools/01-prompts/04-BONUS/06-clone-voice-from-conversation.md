# Prompt: Clone Your Voice from a Conversation

Your writing samples show how you write when you're being careful. But your real voice lives in your messages: WhatsApp, Slack, emails you typed fast, voice notes you transcribed.

This prompt extracts your natural communication patterns from a real conversation and updates your voice DNA with the way you actually talk. The result: agents that sound like you on a Tuesday morning, not you on a press release.

Copy and paste this into your chat:

---

```
I want to improve my voice DNA by learning from how I actually communicate in real conversations.

---
Source: [WhatsApp thread / Slack messages / Email chain / Voice note transcript / Other]
Context: [Who was I talking to? What was the situation?]

My messages from the conversation:
[PASTE YOUR MESSAGES HERE. Include only YOUR messages, not the other person's. If it's a thread, mark which messages are yours.]
---

Please do the following:

1. Read C-core/voice-dna.md to understand what the system already knows about my voice

2. Analyze my conversation messages and identify:
   - **Sentence patterns:** How long are my sentences? Do I use fragments? Do I start with "So" or "Look" or "The thing is"?
   - **Word choices:** What words do I repeat? What words do I never use? Do I use slang, Hebrew words mixed in, or industry jargon?
   - **Tone markers:** Am I direct or soft? Do I use humor? Am I formal or casual? How do I open and close?
   - **Rhythm:** Do I use short punchy sentences or flowing ones? Do I list things? Do I use questions?
   - **Emotional patterns:** How do I express excitement? Disagreement? Urgency? How do I say no?
   - **Conversational habits:** Do I use "btw", "tbh", emojis, exclamation marks? What's my texting style?

3. Compare what you found with the current voice-dna.md:
   - What patterns are missing from voice-dna?
   - What patterns in voice-dna don't match how I actually talk?
   - What's the gap between my "formal writing voice" and my "real voice"?

4. Update C-core/voice-dna.md with a new section called "Natural Voice Patterns" that captures:
   - How I actually open messages
   - My go-to phrases and expressions
   - My rhythm and sentence structure in casual mode
   - Things I do naturally that the system should mimic

5. Update A-agents/copywriter-agent.md with specific examples from the conversation that show "this is how the owner actually sounds"

6. Update M-memory/learning-log.md with what was learned

Show me the before/after comparison of what changed in voice-dna.md.
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- **Paste at least 10-15 of your messages.** More is better. The system needs enough data to find patterns.
- **Pick conversations where you were being yourself.** Not the one where you were being extra polite with a new client. The one where you were talking to a friend, a team member, or a long-time partner.
- **Mix contexts.** One excited conversation + one where you disagreed + one casual check-in = a full picture of your voice.
- **Include messy messages.** Typos, half-sentences, emoji usage. That's the real you. That's what makes the voice authentic.
