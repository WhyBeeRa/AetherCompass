# Prompt: Enhance Your Agent with Deep Research

You just created an agent. Now make it world-class.

This prompt generates a deep research query you can copy into Google Gemini Deep Research (or any AI research tool). The research will come back with everything your agent needs to become a top 1% expert in its domain: skills, frameworks, tools, mindset, common mistakes, and insider knowledge.

You paste the research back into your agent file, and suddenly your marketing agent thinks like a CMO, your sales agent closes like a top performer, and your HR agent operates like a VP of People.

Copy and paste this into your chat (fill in the agent details first):

---

```
I want to make one of my agents significantly better by feeding it expert-level knowledge about its domain.

---
Agent name: [e.g., "copywriter", "researcher", "strategist", or a new agent you just created]
Agent's role: [What does this agent do? e.g., "writes marketing content", "analyzes business decisions", "manages HR communication"]
Domain: [The professional field, e.g., "content marketing", "B2B sales", "HR and people operations", "product management"]
---

Please do the following:

1. Read the agent file in A-agents/ to understand its current capabilities

2. Read C-core/project-brief.md to understand the business context

3. Generate a deep research prompt I can paste into Google Gemini Deep Research (or any AI research tool). The prompt should ask:

   - What makes someone a top 1% [role] in [domain]?
   - What specific skills separate great from good in this field?
   - What frameworks, mental models, and decision-making approaches do the best practitioners use?
   - What tools, techniques, and workflows do top performers rely on?
   - What mindset, habits, and principles define the elite in this domain?
   - What are the most common mistakes and blind spots, even among experienced practitioners?
   - What does the latest thinking (2024-2026) say about where this field is heading?
   - What would a world-class [role] know that most people in the field don't?

   Make the prompt specific to MY business context (from C-core/project-brief.md), not generic. The research should come back with knowledge that directly improves how my agent performs for MY use case.

4. Format the research prompt as a clean block I can copy-paste directly into Gemini Deep Research. No extra formatting, no instructions around it. Just the prompt itself, ready to paste.

5. After I paste the research results back here, help me integrate the best findings into the agent file:
   - Add a "Domain Expertise" section with the most valuable insights
   - Update the agent's checklists with expert-level quality checks
   - Add specific frameworks or mental models the agent should use
   - Update any skills in T-tools/02-skills/ that this agent uses

Show me the research prompt first. I'll run it externally and come back with the results.
```

---

## How to Use This

1. Run the prompt above in Claude Code. You'll get a research query.
2. Copy the research query and paste it into [Google Gemini Deep Research](https://gemini.google.com/) (or any AI research tool that does deep research).
3. Wait for the research to finish (usually a few minutes).
4. Copy the research results back into Claude Code and say: "Here are the research results. Please integrate them into my agent."
5. Claude updates your agent file with expert-level knowledge.

**The result:** An agent that doesn't just follow instructions, but thinks like a domain expert.

---

## Examples

**Marketing agent:**
"What makes a marketing expert top 1% in their domain? What skills, tools, mindset, frameworks, and knowledge would help a marketing agent become genuinely world-class?"

**HR agent:**
"What makes an HR/People Operations leader top 1% in their field? What approaches to employee communication, conflict resolution, culture building, and talent development separate the best from the rest?"

**Sales agent:**
"What makes a B2B sales professional top 1%? What discovery frameworks, objection handling techniques, relationship building strategies, and closing approaches do the best in the world use?"

**Product manager agent:**
"What makes a product manager top 1%? What prioritization frameworks, stakeholder management techniques, user research methods, and strategic thinking patterns define the elite PMs?"
